# Revenue Funnel

> Every layer makes money. Every layer feeds the next. The funnel is a loop.

---

## The Four Layers

| Layer | Service | Revenue | Feeds |
|---|---|---|---|
| [[Layer 1 — Assess]] | [[Commercial Inspections]] | Fee per inspection | Layer 2, 3, or 4 |
| [[Layer 2 — Plan]] | [[Preconstruction and Estimating]] | Fee per estimate | Layer 3 or 4 |
| [[Layer 3 — Advise]] | [[Risk Consulting]] | Project fee / retainer | Layer 4 |
| [[Layer 4 — Protect]] | [[NestShield Technology]] | Install + monthly recurring | Back to Layer 1 |

## How It Works

You lead with the client's problem, not your product. Whatever they need right now — that's the entry point. You solve it, get paid, and your deliverable surfaces the next problem.

**Nobody is being "sold down the funnel."** The work itself creates the case for the next step. If an inspection doesn't find anything that needs estimating, the client stays at Layer 1. That's fine. You got paid.

## Entry Points

Clients enter wherever their pain is:
- "I'm buying a building" → [[Layer 1 — Assess]]
- "I'm bidding a project" → [[Layer 2 — Plan]]
- "My insurance company is asking questions" → [[Layer 3 — Advise]]
- "We keep having water losses" → [[Layer 4 — Protect]]

## The Loop

[[NestShield Technology]] monitoring data feeds back to the top:
- Sensor data shows system degradation → new inspection
- Alerts approaching failure → estimate for replacement
- Patterns across portfolio → new risk engagement
- Client acquires new building → starts at Layer 1

## Rules

- Every layer is priced to be profitable standalone. No loss leaders.
- Never pitch. Show findings. The next step sells itself.
- Every deliverable solves the current problem AND surfaces the next one.
