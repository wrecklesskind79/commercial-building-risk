# Layer 1 — Assess

**Service:** [[Commercial Inspections]]
**Division:** [[Commercial Building Risk]]
**Revenue:** Flat fee per inspection

---

## Entry Triggers

- Networking: "I'm looking at a property" / "We're doing due diligence"
- Referral from broker, lender, attorney, insurance agent
- Direct inquiry from website or LinkedIn
- Property manager: "We need a condition assessment"
- Investor: "We want to know what we're buying"

## Step-by-Step SOP

### 1. Qualify (5 min, first call)

- Property type? ([[Multifamily]], [[Hotels]], [[Office]], [[Industrial]], [[Retail]], [[Healthcare]], [[Education]])
- Size? (SF, unit count, floors)
- Purpose? (Acquisition, insurance, maintenance planning, litigation, refinance)
- Timeline? (Due diligence deadlines are hard — get the date)
- Decision-maker? (Buyer, broker, or someone who can't authorize?)

**Go/No-Go:** Property in your wheelhouse + timeline works + talking to decision-maker = go.

### 2. Scope and Price (within 24 hours)

Written scope including:
- Property address and type
- What's covered / what's excluded
- Deliverable format and delivery timeline
- Flat fee price
- Terms (payment on delivery, cancellation policy)

### 3. Schedule and Prep

- Confirm property access
- Request existing documentation (plans, prior reports, maintenance records, loss history)
- Review Google Earth / street view
- Build property-type-specific checklist

### 4. Inspect

- Exterior first: roof, envelope, site drainage, parking
- Photograph everything — over-document
- Follow checklist but stay alert for what's NOT on it
- Talk to on-site staff — they know where the problems are
- Note everything that represents **ongoing risk**, not just current defects
- Water intrusion, system age, missing shutoffs = future [[NestShield Technology]] opportunities

### 5. Write Report (3–5 business days)

- Executive summary (1 page)
- Findings by system, severity-rated (critical / significant / maintenance / monitor)
- Each finding: description, location, photos, severity
- Cost opinions (ranges, not hard bids)
- Recommended actions with priority
- **Risk exposure summary** — the handoff section

### 6. Deliver and Discuss

Walk the client through it. Never just email it.
