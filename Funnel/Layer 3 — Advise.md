# Layer 3 — Advise

**Service:** [[Risk Consulting]]
**Division:** [[Commercial Building Risk]]
**Revenue:** Project fee or retainer

---

## Entry Triggers

- Handoff from [[Layer 2 — Plan]] (estimate revealed ongoing risk)
- Handoff from [[Layer 1 — Assess]] (inspection found systemic risk, skipped estimating)
- Direct: insurance company wants a risk assessment
- Referral: insurance broker, attorney, lender
- Client's loss history (claims rising, premiums increasing)

## Step-by-Step SOP

### 1. Define Engagement (30–45 min call)

- What's driving this? (Losses, premium increases, lender requirement, operational problems)
- Risk type? (Water, fire, mechanical, envelope, operational, environmental)
- Portfolio? (Single building or multiple — risk consulting scales)
- Documentation? (Loss runs, maintenance records, inspection reports)
- Outcome needed? (Risk report, maintenance plan, insurance submission, board presentation)

### 2. Collect and Review Data

Before going on-site:
- Insurance loss history (5 years minimum)
- Prior inspection/assessment reports (yours from [[Layer 1 — Assess|Layer 1]])
- Prior estimates (yours from [[Layer 2 — Plan|Layer 2]])
- Maintenance logs and work orders
- BAS data if available
- Utility bills

**Look for patterns.** Three water losses in 2 years = systemic problem, not bad luck.

### 3. Conduct Risk Assessment (on-site)

- Walk every system related to identified risk categories
- Interview maintenance staff and property management
- Document age, condition, failure modes of critical systems
- Map water supply, drainage, shutoff locations
- Identify single points of failure
- Assess emergency response capability
- **Document what's NOT there** — missing shutoffs, no leak detection, no monitoring

### 4. Write Risk Report

- Executive summary with **total risk exposure in dollars**
- Risk inventory ranked by likelihood × financial impact
- Root cause analysis for recurring losses
- Maintenance plan with costs and timelines
- **Technology recommendations** — the handoff section
- Cost-benefit: prevention vs. next loss
- Insurance impact

### 5. Deliver and Discuss

Present in person or detailed call.
- Start with the money: "Your building is carrying $X in unmitigated risk"
- Top 3–5 exposures
- Maintenance plan as baseline
- Technology as force multiplier

## Handoff to Next Layer

See [[Handoff Scripts#Layer 3 to Layer 4]].

**Triggers:** Water risk exposure, freeze exposure, water loss history, no existing leak detection or auto-shutoff, insurance pressure, high-value contents/operations.

**The math:** Their worst water loss vs. annual [[NestShield Technology]] cost. If one prevented loss = 3–5 years of monitoring, the decision is obvious.

**If no handoff needed:** Invoice, deliver maintenance plan, schedule 6-month check-in.
