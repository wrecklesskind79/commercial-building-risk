# Career Classes

Seven levels. Every position in [[NestShield]] maps to one. The class defines what the person owns, what they can decide, and what gets them to the next level.

---

## Class 1 — Trainee

**What they do:** Learn the work. Follow instructions. Build habits.
**Decision authority:** None solo. Executes assigned tasks.
**Owns:** Their own learning curve. Showing up ready. Asking questions before assuming.
**Leadership expected:** Zero. Be coachable.
**Experience:** 0–1 years
**Promotion to Class 2:** Complete standard tasks independently. Pass entry-level certs.
**Comp:** Entry-level. Hourly or low-end salary. They're an investment.

**Positions:** Estimating Technician I, Inspection Technician I, Installation Technician I, Monitoring Coordinator, Sales Development Rep

---

## Class 2 — Technician / Coordinator

**What they do:** Execute core work reliably without hand-holding. Handle standard situations. Flag non-standard.
**Decision authority:** Routine decisions within established procedures. Escalates the rest.
**Owns:** Task completion quality. Own schedule. Basic client communication.
**Leadership expected:** Can orient a Trainee.
**Experience:** 1–3 years
**Promotion to Class 3:** Consistent quality. Handles 80% without escalation. Division certs complete.
**Comp:** Market rate. No longer an investment — they're producing.

**Positions:** Estimating Technician II, Inspection Technician II, Installation Technician II, Client Success Specialist, Business Development Rep

---

## Class 3 — Specialist

**What they do:** Own outcomes, not just tasks. Solve problems. Clients trust them directly.
**Decision authority:** Judgment calls on own work. Commits to scope/timeline on standard projects.
**Owns:** Project-level outcomes. Client relationships. Deliverable quality.
**Leadership expected:** Informally mentors juniors. Can run a site or project alone.
**Experience:** 3–6 years
**Promotion to Class 4:** Demonstrated expertise. Handles complex/non-standard. Client satisfaction track record. Advanced cert.
**Comp:** Above-market for strong performers. Hardest to replace.

**Positions:** Estimator I/II, Commercial Inspector I/II, Risk Analyst, Systems Specialist, Sr. Client Success Specialist, Account Executive

---

## Class 4 — Senior Specialist / Project Lead

**What they do:** Handle the hardest work. Set the standard. Either the go-to expert OR leading small teams.
**Decision authority:** Full domain authority. Commits resources within budget. Client-facing decisions that stick.
**Owns:** Quality standards. Complex project outcomes. Training and development below them. Client retention.
**Leadership expected:** [[Technical Track]]: defines best practices, writes procedures, trains, final word on technical questions. [[Leadership Track]]: runs a team of 3–8 day-to-day.
**Experience:** 5–10 years
**Promotion to Class 5:** Technical: recognized expert, improved systems, others seek them out. Leadership: built a team, managed P&L/budget, developed someone who got promoted.
**Comp:** Senior-level. Significant differentiation on impact. Outcome-based bonuses.

**Positions:** Senior Estimator, Senior Inspector, Senior Risk Consultant, Senior Systems Specialist, Project Lead, Account Manager, Sales Manager

---

## Class 5 — Manager

**What they do:** Run a function or department. Responsible for team output. Hires, fires, develops.
**Decision authority:** Hires/terminates (exec approval on termination). Sets team priorities. Owns department budget.
**Owns:** Department performance. Team development. Process improvement. Client satisfaction across area. Budget.
**Leadership expected:** Full people management. Reviews. Career conversations. Conflict resolution. Cross-dept coordination.
**Experience:** 7–12 years
**Promotion to Class 6:** Department consistently hits targets. Team functions without them. Budget managed responsibly. Strategic thinking.
**Comp:** Management premium. Base + performance bonus on dept metrics. Potential equity/profit-sharing.

**Positions:** Estimating Manager, Inspection Manager, Risk Services Manager, Regional Manager, Client Success Manager, Sales Manager

---

## Class 6 — Director

**What they do:** Own a business line or multiple departments. Set strategy. P&L responsibility.
**Decision authority:** Sets strategy. Approves budgets. Org structure decisions. Represents company externally. Commits to partnerships/contracts.
**Owns:** Revenue and profitability. Multi-year planning. Org design. Key client relationships. Market positioning.
**Leadership expected:** Builds and leads Managers. Develops next-gen leadership. Drives culture. Makes hard calls.
**Experience:** 10–15 years
**Promotion to Class 7:** Proven P&L. Scaled a team or line. Industry reputation. Strategic moves that changed the company.
**Comp:** Executive-adjacent. Significant variable comp on business performance. Equity.

**Positions:** Director of Preconstruction, Director of Building Assessments, Director of Risk Services, Director of Field Operations, Director of Monitoring Services, Director of Sales

---

## Class 7 — Executive

**What they do:** Run the company or a major segment. Set vision. Make bets. Own the whole outcome.
**Decision authority:** Everything not reserved for board/owner. Capital allocation. Strategic direction. Org-wide policy.
**Owns:** Company performance. Culture. Strategy. External reputation.
**Leadership expected:** Leads leaders. Builds exec team. Decides with incomplete information. Takes responsibility publicly.
**Experience:** 15+ years
**How you get here:** You don't get promoted. You earn it by building something, or you're recruited.
**Comp:** Highly variable. Base + significant incentive + equity tied to company valuation/growth.

**Positions:** CRO, COO, VP of Operations, General Manager
