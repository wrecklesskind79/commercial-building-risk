# Technical Track

> Advance by getting better at the work. No management required.

**Path:** Trainee → Technician → Specialist → Senior Specialist → Principal Specialist → Technical Director

---

| Level | What Changes | Authority | Cert Expectation | Business Contribution |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Trainee | Learning the work | None | Entry-level in progress | Costs money (investment) |
| Technician | Doing the work | Routine decisions | Entry-level complete | Breaks even |
| Specialist | Owning outcomes | Judgment calls on own work | 1+ advanced | Generates revenue |
| Senior Specialist | Setting the standard | Full domain authority | Multiple advanced | Retains clients, trains others |
| Principal Specialist | Defining the discipline | Company-wide technical authority | Industry-recognized | Creates IP, wins complex bids |
| Technical Director | Driving technical strategy | Strategic technical decisions | Thought leader | Shapes market position |

## The Rule

Technical track people never manage anyone to advance. Their value is expertise, not headcount. They can switch to the [[Leadership Track]] at any equivalent level if they want to.
